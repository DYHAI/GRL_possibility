\subsection{The conditional mean is not a solution of nonlinear dynamics}
\label{subsec:nonclosure}

\begin{proposition}[Conditional expectation is not a nonlinear physical solution]
\label{prop:nonlinear_nonclosure}
Let $u_t$ be a random state generated by a nonlinear dynamical system
$\partial_t u = \mathcal{N}(u)$,
where $\mathcal{N}$ is nonlinear.
Suppose every sample path satisfies this equation, and define the one-step conditional mean
$\bar u_{t+\Delta t}=\mathbb{E}[u_{t+\Delta t}\mid u_t]$.
If the conditional distribution of $u_{t+\Delta t}$ given $u_t$ is not almost surely degenerate,
then $\bar u_{t+\Delta t}$ is not, in general, a solution of $\partial_t u=\mathcal{N}(u)$. Instead,
\begin{equation}
    \partial_t \bar u_{t+\Delta t}
    =
    \mathcal{N}(\bar u_{t+\Delta t}) + R_{t+\Delta t},
    \qquad
    R_{t+\Delta t}\neq 0 \text{ in general}.
    \label{eq:closure_error}
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Taking conditional expectation of $\partial_t u_{t+\Delta t}=\mathcal{N}(u_{t+\Delta t})$ yields
$\partial_t \bar u_{t+\Delta t}=\mathbb{E}[\mathcal{N}(u_{t+\Delta t})\mid u_t]$.
Exact nonlinear closure would require
$\mathbb{E}[\mathcal{N}(u_{t+\Delta t})\mid u_t]=\mathcal{N}(\bar u_{t+\Delta t})$,
which fails whenever $\mathcal{N}$ is nonlinear and the conditional distribution is non-degenerate.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Jensen's inequality and conserved nonlinear functionals]
\label{rem:jensen}
For a convex nonlinear functional $f$ (e.g., kinetic energy $f(u)=|u|^2$),
Jensen's inequality gives
$\mathbb{E}[f(u_{t+\Delta t})\mid u_t]\ge f(\mathbb{E}[u_{t+\Delta t}\mid u_t])$
(with equality only for degenerate conditional laws).
Thus evaluating a nonlinear conserved quantity on the RMSE-optimal mean field
$\bar u_{t+\Delta t}$ does not equal the conditional expectation of that quantity along sample paths.
For the compressible Euler system with nonlinear flux $\mathbf{F}(\mathbf{U})$,
the same logic applies componentwise: the conditional mean of conservative variables is generally
not a weak solution of the original PDE.
\end{remark}

This provides a structural explanation for \emph{violation of conservation laws} by RMSE-optimal deterministic AI forecasts: the model target is $\mathbb{E}[\mathbf{X}\mid\cdot]$, not a single physically consistent trajectory.
